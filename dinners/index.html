Our current book is Peru: The Cookbook. We thought we'd dive into our different heritages, starting out with a trip to South America.

Really looking forward to this one!

Six Seasons: A New Way with Vegetables by Joshua McFadden was our first cookbook pick. This plant-based cookbook has quickly become one of my favorite cookbooks of all time. The menu:

  • Pasta alla Gricia with Slivered Sugar Snap Peas
  • Potato and Cauliflower with Olives, Feta, and Arugula
  • Toasted (Homemade) Bread with Whipped Ricotta, Ramps, and Raisins

Our next book was Vietnamese Food Any Day by Andrea Nguyen. We all loved her story and found her style unintimidating and inviting. The menu:

  • Turmeric Fish, Seared Dill, and Green Onion Noodle Bowls
  • Roasted Cauliflower Wings
  • Green Mango Cabbage Jicama Salad
  • Streetside Corn and Chile Salad
  • Shiitake Tofu Frittata
  • Turmeric Coconut Rice
  • Vietnamese Coffee Ice Cream

Next we dipped into Nopalito: A Mexican Kitchen by Gonzalo Guzman. Let's be honest, I want to cook every single recipe in this book. It's that good. Our menu:

  • Sweet Potato Tamales
  • Veggie Enchiladas
  • Black Bean Gorditas
  • Salsa de Arbol and Guacamole
  • Margaritas

The last dinner we had pre-pandemic was Cork and Knife: Build Complex Flavors with Bourbon, Wine, Beer and More by Emily and Matt Clifton. This is the perfect cozy winter cookbook! Warmed us all up. Our menu:

  • Roasted Tofu and Butternut Squash Curry
  • Bourbon Sloppy Joes with Pickled Onions
  • Spinach Risotto
  • Red Wine Raspberry Plum Galette

After taking a few months off, we returned with a backyard dinner for Indian-ish: Recipes and Antics from a Modern American Family by Priya Krishna. This book was a fun read with and easy way to make comfort Indian food. Our menu:

  • Saag Paneer and Matar Paneer
  • Rice Noodle Poha
  • Kachumber Tilapia with Cilantro Chutney
  • Basmati Rice
  • Cardamom Bread Pudding
